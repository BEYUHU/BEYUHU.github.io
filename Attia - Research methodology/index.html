Reading Journal Paper

1. Audience for this method

  • Who wants to know: How to read research papers
  • Undergrad and graduate students who want to read a paper efficiently

2. Before reading it:

  • Reading is a key skill of any graduate
  • Scientific articles keep you on top of ideas
  • Different levels to reading proficiency:
    LEVERL 1: Cognitive skills
    LEVERL 2: Reading fluency
    LEVERL 3: Reading comprehension
    LEVERL 4: Reading with metacognition
  • Understand the structure of article:
    1. Title
    2. Abstract
    3. Keywords
    4. Main text (IMRAD)
      • Introduction
      • Methods
      • Results
      • And
      • Discussions
    5. Conclusion
    6. …

2.1 READING STEPS (within 2 hours)

1st Reading: Skim the article (30 mins)

  • Abstract
  • Introduction
  • Conclusion

2nd Reading: Serious reading (30 mins)

  • Deeply introduction
  • Deeply results and discussion (some unprofessional paper will mix up both, need to separate them)
  • Check the figures
  • Skim the rest

3rd Reading: Serious reading (60 mins)

  • Answer questions:
    What did the author try to accomplish?
    How was it accomplished?
  • NB:
    1. timer setting: motivate to finish
    2. take notes on separate medium

2.2 Techniques

Summarize:

  • Read 1st and the last sentence of the paragraph
  • Highlight important words
  • Short summary at the end of paragraph / page / chapter

Learn:

  • The methodology used (sometimes ‘how it was done’ is more important than ‘what it was done’), good paper will be very clear
  • Make deduction based on inference
  • Use it or loose it (cite the paper in an engaged way, to truly understand by criticizing it)

Habit/structure

  • Read on print (DON’T do it on digital device)
  • Use color pencils
  • Create your own structure

2.3 Summary

  • It’s all about comprehension and retention
  • Read on print
  • Create your own habit / beat cognitive impatience
  • Summarize and take notes
  • Be critical and identify gaps for high cognitive retention or you loose it
